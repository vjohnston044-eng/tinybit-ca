What the research shows

Dr. Rhonda Patrick, FoundMyFitness

Menopause involves a dramatic shift in estrogen and progesterone, which impacts mitochondrial function, metabolic rate, and nutrient absorption.

Strategic protein and micronutrient intake become non-negotiable.
Research: Amino acid status in menopause

Plant-based diets alone may not provide sufficient bioavailable forms of key micronutrients (B12, iron, zinc) especially during menopause when absorption changes.

Complementing plant foods with targeted animal proteins improves nutrient density and status.
Study: Recovery from disordered eating

Nutritional rehabilitation benefits from real, nutrient-dense foods. The psychological aspect of permission and normalcy around food accelerates recovery.

Documented food choice matters as much as calories.
